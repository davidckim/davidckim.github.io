What is Pair Programming?

Pair programming consist of a driver and navigator, where both individuals are helping each other out and learning from each other as they code. They both sit at one workstation to collaborate and switch roles throughout the project.

Why is Pair Programming Important to DBC?

Pair programming is important to DBC because it is the main culture at DBC. Pair programming plays a critcal role in the software industry overall. It is one the most efficient ways to learn from your peers, colleagues, and friends. It helps the pair stay motivated throughout the project, and it makes it a lot easier to pinpoint mistakes/problems.

Compare Driver and Navigator

Both roles have their own tasks, but they depend on each other in order for it to work. If one needs help or runs into a roadblock then the other can step in and help at anypoint of the journey. The driver is the one who works the steering wheel and pedals as the navigator guides the driver when to turn or when to stop.
